Nelson R. Mandela

"The greatest glory in living lies not in never falling, but in rising every time we fall."

Here's a time line of Nelson Mandela's life:

  • 1918 – Born on July 18 in Mvezo, a small village in the Eastern Cape of South Africa.
  • 1939 – Begins law studies at the University of Fort Hare, but is expelled for joining a student protest.
  • 1941 – Moves to Johannesburg and works as a mine security officer while studying law part-time.
  • 1943 – Joins the African National Congress (ANC), becoming active in the struggle against apartheid.
  • 1944 – Co-founds the ANC Youth League, promoting more radical tactics to end racial discrimination.
  • 1952 – Launches the Defiance Campaign, encouraging nonviolent resistance to apartheid laws.
  • 1956 – Arrested and charged with treason along with 155 others; acquitted in 1961.
  • 1961 – Helps found Umkhonto we Sizwe (Spear of the Nation), the armed wing of the ANC.
  • 1962 – Arrested while traveling abroad; sentenced to five years for leaving the country illegally and inciting workers.
  • 1964 – Sentenced to life imprisonment during the Rivonia Trial for sabotage and conspiracy against the government.
  • 1964–1990 – Imprisoned mostly on Robben Island, enduring harsh conditions but becoming a symbol of resistance.
  • 1976 – Soweto Uprising against apartheid sparks global awareness and pressure for Mandela’s release.
  • 1985 – Rejects a government offer for conditional release, insisting on full abolition of apartheid.
  • 1990 – Released from prison on February 11 after 27 years behind bars.
  • 1991 – Elected President of the ANC as the organization is unbanned.
  • 1993 – Shares the Nobel Peace Prize with F.W. de Klerk for their efforts to peacefully end apartheid.
  • 1994 – Becomes South Africa’s first Black president, overseeing the transition to democracy.
  • 2013 – dies at the age of 95.
